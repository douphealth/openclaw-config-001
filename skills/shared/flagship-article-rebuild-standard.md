# Flagship Article Rebuild Standard

## Purpose
Force weak articles to be rebuilt as competitive flagship assets instead of lightly refreshed outlines.

## When to Use
Use when an article suffers from one or more of these:
- thin content
- slug/content mismatch
- weak SERP/title logic
- poor FAQ depth
- weak internal linking
- poor GEO/AEO quality
- weak human authority signals
- excessive CTA/noise
- weak visuals or cluttered HTML

## Mandatory Rebuild Requirements
A flagship rebuild should address all relevant layers:
1. slug/topic alignment review
2. 301 redirect recommendation if slug mismatch is severe
3. title tag quality and length review
4. stronger meta description
5. 2,000–4,000 word target where topic breadth justifies it
6. stronger hook in the first 100 words
7. direct answer blocks in major sections
8. keyword-rich H2/H3 hierarchy
9. 5–8 PAA-style FAQs when topic supports it
10. contextual internal links in body, not just bottom lists
11. cited-source plan or clearly scoped non-fabricated authority framing
12. human-quality signals: examples, experience framing, edge cases, contrarian insight
13. cleaner HTML and lower content-to-noise ratio
14. schema recommendations
15. cluster role and topical authority role
16. FAQ answers expanded to snippet-friendly 40–60 word range where appropriate
17. key entities bolded on first strong use when it improves scanability
18. related guides aligned to the correct topical cluster, not generic adjacent content
19. visual/alt-text review so imagery supports the actual topic
20. CTA/noise review so the reading flow is not repeatedly interrupted
21. References section with genuinely helpful, clickable, high-quality resources where external grounding improves trust or usefulness
22. modern premium HTML composition using safe callouts, summary cards, cleaner visual hierarchy, and elegant reading flow without fragile markup

## UX Rules
- reduce repetitive CTAs
- reduce image spam
- prioritize reading flow over decorative interruptions
- keep paragraphs scannable
- use lists/tables intentionally

## GEO / AEO Rules
- include snippet-ready answers
- definitions in clean prose
- quotable paragraphs
- PAA coverage
- entity-rich language where relevant
- no vague outline-only sections

## Human Quality Rules
- no fake stats
- no fake sources
- no fake case studies
- no AI filler phrases
- no generic mystical fluff
- no shallow rewrites pretending to be comprehensive

## Verification Checklist
Before claiming complete, confirm:
- topic depth is materially better
- headings are stronger
- internal linking is stronger
- FAQ depth is stronger
- slug issue assessed
- CTA clutter reduced where possible
- content is clearly more competitive than before
