## 2. Content Hierarchy & Page Types

### 2.1 Money Pages (Direct Revenue)

#### "Best [Product] for [Use Case]" — Roundup Reviews

**Template structure:**
```
Title: Best [Product Category] for [Use Case] in [Year]
URL: /best-[product-category]-for-[use-case]/
Word count: 3000–6000

Sections:
1. Quick Picks (top 3 with one-line summary)
2. Detailed Reviews (5–10 products, 300–500 words each)
   - Product name + image
   - Key specs (table format)
   - Pros (3–5 bullet points)
   - Cons (2–3 bullet points)
   - Best for: [specific use case]
   - Pricing
   - CTA button/link
3. Comparison Table (all products side by side)
4. Buying Guide (how we tested, what to look for)
5. FAQ (PAA questions)
6. Final Verdict (clear winner + runner-ups by use case)
```

**Winning patterns:**
- Use sub-headings for each product: "Best for X," "Best Budget," "Best Premium"
- Include real screenshots or images of products
- Show pricing prominently (readers want fast comparison)
- Have a clear "Our Pick" or "Editor's Choice" badge
- Mobile-friendly comparison table

#### "[Product A] vs [Product B]" — Comparison Pages

**Template structure:**
```
Title: [Product A] vs [Product B]: Which Is Better for [Use Case]?
URL: /[product-a]-vs-[product-b]/
Word count: 2000–4000

Sections:
1. Quick Verdict (TL;DR — who wins and why)
2. At-a-Glance Comparison Table
3. Feature-by-Feature Comparison (3–5 features)
   - Feature name
   - Product A: description + rating
   - Product B: description + rating
   - Winner: A or B
4. Pricing Comparison
5. Pros and Cons (each product)
6. Who Should Choose [Product A]?
7. Who Should Choose [Product B]?
8. FAQ
9. Final Verdict
```

**Conversion tips:**
- Include affiliate links in the verdict section
- Use CTA buttons, not just text links
- "Try [Product A] Free" converts better than "Visit [Product A]"
- Place first affiliate link within the first 300 words (below the quick verdict)

#### "[Product] Review" — Single Product Deep-Dives

**Template structure:**
```
Title: [Product] Review ([Year]): [Honest/In-Depth] Analysis
URL: /[product]-review/
Word count: 1500–3000

Sections:
1. Overall Rating (visual star rating)
2. Key Pros and Cons (scannable)
3. Who Is [Product] For?
4. Who Is [Product] NOT For?
5. Features Deep-Dive (3–5 key features)
6. Pricing Breakdown
7. Hands-On Experience (screenshots, personal testing)
8. Alternatives (2–3 competitors)
9. FAQ
10. Final Verdict + CTA
```

**Trust signals:**
- Include "Last Updated: [Date]" prominently
- Mention if you've actually used the product
- Include both pros AND cons (readers detect one-sided reviews)
- Link to alternatives (shows you're not just pushing one product)

### 2.2 Supporting Pages (Authority + Internal Links)

#### "How to Choose [Product Category]" — Buying Guides

**Purpose:** Rank for informational/commercial queries and funnel readers to money pages.

**Structure:**
```
1. What to Look For (3–5 key criteria)
2. Feature explanations (what each feature means)
3. Common mistakes to avoid
4. Price ranges and what you get at each
5. Our recommendations (link to roundup review)
6. Checklist readers can use
```

#### "[Product] Alternatives" Pages

**Purpose:** Capture traffic from people researching but not committed.

**Structure:**
```
1. Why Look for Alternatives? (address the objection)
2. Top 5 Alternatives (brief descriptions)
3. Comparison Table
4. When to Switch vs Stay
5. Detailed Alternative Reviews
```

### 2.3 Top-Funnel Pages (Traffic + Email Capture)

**Purpose:** Build organic traffic, capture emails, establish authority.

**Types:**
- "What is [Concept]?" (definitional, targets featured snippets)
- "[Topic] Statistics" (linkable assets, data-driven)
- "[Topic] Mistakes to Avoid" (listicles, high engagement)
- "[Topic] Checklist" (lead magnets for email capture)

**Monetization approach:**
- Soft-sell with contextual affiliate links (not aggressive)
- Include "Related: Our Top Picks for [Category]" sidebar/CTA
- Email capture → nurture sequence → money page links

## 3. Internal Linking Strategy for Affiliate Sites

### Link Flow Rules

```
Money pages → link to: other money pages, buying guides, alternatives
Buying guides → link to: money pages (primary), informational content
Informational → link to: buying guides (primary), money pages (secondary)
Alternatives → link to: the product being replaced, related money pages
Reviews → link to: comparison pages, alternatives, buying guides
```

### Anchor Text Guidelines

| From → To | Anchor Pattern | Example |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Buying guide → Roundup | Exact match or close variant | "best WordPress hosting" |
| Info page → Buying guide | Descriptive, natural | "our hosting recommendations" |
| Review → Comparison | Descriptive | "how it stacks up against competitors" |
| Any → Review | Product name | "read our Bluehost review" |
| Any → Alternatives | Natural phrasing | "other options worth considering" |

### CTA Placement

Money pages should have affiliate CTAs at:
1. After the quick verdict (first CTA, highest visibility)
2. At the end of each product review
3. In the comparison table (linked product names)
4. After the buying guide section
5. At the very end (final CTA)

**Do NOT:**
- Place more than one CTA per 500 words
- Use aggressive pop-ups or sticky CTAs
- Hide affiliate links in misleading anchor text
- Put CTAs before providing value (build trust first)

## 4. Review Methodology & Trust Building

### 4.1 Review Depth Requirements

| Review Type | Minimum Words | Products Tested | Screenshots | Video |
|------------|---------------|-----------------|-------------|-------|
| Single product | 1500 | 1 | 3+ | Optional |
| Roundup (5–10) | 3000 | All listed | 1 per product | Optional |
| Roundup (10+) | 5000 | Top 5 tested, rest researched | Top 5 only | Recommended |
| Comparison | 2000 | Both | 2+ per product | Recommended |

### 4.2 Review Credibility Signals

**Must-have:**
- [ ] Hands-on evidence (screenshots, personal account, test data)
- [ ] Pros AND cons (balanced assessment)
- [ ] Specific use case recommendations
- [ ] Pricing transparency (actual costs, including hidden fees)
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure (FTC compliant, visible)
- [ ] Last updated date
- [ ] Clear rating methodology

**Nice-to-have:**
- [ ] Video walkthrough
- [ ] User review aggregation
- [ ] Performance benchmarks
- [ ] Side-by-side screenshots
- [ ] Customer support test results

### 4.3 Rating Methodology

Define a clear, repeatable rating system:

```python
# Example: 5-dimension rating system
rating_dimensions = {
    "features": {
        "weight": 0.25,
        "description": "Breadth and depth of features"
    },
    "ease_of_use": {
        "weight": 0.20,
        "description": "User experience and learning curve"
    },
    "value_for_money": {
        "weight": 0.25,
        "description": "Price relative to features and competitors"
    },
    "support": {
        "weight": 0.15,
        "description": "Customer support quality and availability"
    },
    "reliability": {
        "weight": 0.15,
        "description": "Uptime, performance, track record"
    }
}

def calculate_overall_rating(scores: dict[str, float]) -> float:
    """
    scores: {dimension: score_0_to_10}
    Returns: weighted average out of 10
    """
    total = 0
    for dim, score in scores.items():
        weight = rating_dimensions[dim]["weight"]
        total += score * weight
    return round(total, 1)
```

## 5. Affiliate Compliance & Disclosure

### FTC Requirements (US)

- Affiliate relationship must be **clearly disclosed**
- Disclosure must be **above the fold** (before first affiliate link)
- Disclosure must be **conspicuous** (not hidden in footer or fine print)
- Use clear language: "We may earn a commission when you buy through our links"

### Recommended Disclosure Placement

```
Option 1: After the first paragraph
"Disclosure: We may earn a commission from links on this page.
This doesn't affect our editorial opinions. [Learn more.]"

Option 2: In a styled box before content
┌──────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 📋 Affiliate Disclosure                      │
│ We independently test and recommend products.│
│ If you buy through our links, we may earn a  │
│ commission at no extra cost to you.          │
└──────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Link Attributes

Always use proper attributes:
```html
<a href="..." rel="nofollow sponsored" target="_blank">Product Name</a>
```

- `nofollow`: Tells search engines not to pass PageRank
- `sponsored`: Identifies paid/affiliate links (Google recommendation)
- `target="_blank"`: Opens in new tab (keeps users on your site)

## 6. Conversion Rate Optimization for Affiliate Content

### High-Converting Patterns

1. **Comparison tables** with "Visit" buttons convert 2–3x better than text links
2. **"Our Pick" or "Editor's Choice"** badges draw clicks to your recommended product
3. **Pricing displayed in tables** reduces bounce rate (readers want quick answers)
4. **Mobile-optimized tables** (stack on small screens) are critical — 60%+ of traffic is mobile
5. **First affiliate link within 300 words** captures readers who skim

### A/B Test Ideas

- CTA button text: "Try Free" vs "Visit Site" vs "Get Started"
- CTA button color: match brand vs high-contrast
- Table layout: all products vs grouped by use case
- Quick verdict placement: top of page vs after intro paragraph
- Number of products: 5 vs 10 vs 15 in roundups

### Tracking Requirements

Every affiliate link should be tagged:
- UTM parameters or affiliate network sub-IDs
- Track clicks, not just conversions
- Monitor CTR by position (top vs middle vs bottom of page)
- Track by content type to find highest RPM formats

|-------------|-------------------|----------------|
