# Funnel Models Reference

## Page Types & Roles

### Homepage
- **Job**: Route visitors to the right service path
- **Content**: Positioning headline, 1–3 service categories, primary CTA to deepest service page
- **NOT the place**: Full service details, pricing breakdowns, 15 testimonials

### Service Page
- **Job**: Sell the next step (inquiry/booking), not the entire service
- **Content**: Problem framing, outcome promise, what's included, proof, CTA
- **Length**: Enough to build trust, short enough to maintain momentum (3–7 scrolls)

### Audit / Assessment Page
- **Job**: Low-risk entry point — buyer gets diagnosis, you get a qualified lead
- **Content**: What's included in the audit, sample output, turnaround time, price, CTA
- **Key**: Specific deliverable (not "we'll review your site" but "12-point conversion audit with 3 prioritized fixes")

### Triage Page
- **Job**: Route different buyer types to different paths
- **Content**: 2–4 buyer-type descriptions with "this is you" language, CTA per type
- **When needed**: When you serve 2+ distinct buyer segments with different services

### Inquiry / Booking Form
- **Job**: Capture enough info to qualify, not enough to scare away
- **Fields**: Name, email, company, one qualifying question (budget/timeline/scope)
- **After submit**: Immediate confirmation + automated next step (calendar, email, call)

## Funnel Patterns

### Simple (1-page path)
```
Homepage (positioning + CTA) → Booking/Inquiry Form → Confirmation
Best for: low-ticket, clear service, low qualification needed
```

### Standard (2-page path)
```
Homepage (route) → Service Page (sell + CTA) → Inquiry Form → Qualification → Booking
Best for: mid-ticket, needs explanation, basic qualification
```

### Audit Entry (3-page path)
```
Homepage (route) → Audit Page (low-risk entry + CTA) → Audit Form → Audit Delivery → Upsell to Service
Best for: high-ticket, complex service, buyer needs to experience your work first
```

### Segmented (4-page path)
```
Homepage (route) → Triage Page (segment) → Type-Specific Service Page → Inquiry → Qualification → Booking
Best for: multiple service lines, different buyer types, larger operation
```

## Conversion Math Cheat Sheet

### Working backwards from revenue
```
Annual revenue target: $X
÷ Average deal size: $Y
= Deals needed: Z
÷ Close rate (qualified → closed): R
= Qualified leads needed: Q
÷ Qualification rate (submission → qualified): P
= Form submissions needed: S
÷ Form conversion rate (visit → submit): F
= Page visits needed: V
```

### Benchmark conversion rates (use as starting point, not truth)

| Step | Benchmark range | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| Visit → CTA click | 3–8% | Homepage; higher on dedicated landing pages |
| CTA click → form start | 40–60% | Drop-off from friction or distraction |
| Form start → submit | 50–70% | Every extra field costs ~10% |
| Submit → qualified | 30–60% | Depends on qualification criteria strictness |
| Qualified → closed | 10–30% | Depends on sales process and deal size |

### Bottleneck identification
- Low page → CTA click: messaging/positioning problem
- Low CTA → form start: CTA-to-form disconnect, slow page, mobile issue
- Low form start → submit: too many fields, trust issue, unclear next step
- Low submit → qualified: qualification criteria too loose or form targeting wrong audience
- Low qualified → closed: sales process problem (not a funnel problem)

## Friction Checklist

- [ ] Form has ≤4 fields
- [ ] CTA button text is specific ("Book your audit" not "Submit")
- [ ] Page loads in <3 seconds
- [ ] Mobile path is thumb-friendly
- [ ] Confirmation page/email exists and is immediate
- [ ] No competing CTAs above the fold
- [ ] Social proof placed near CTA, not buried at bottom
- [ ] No dead ends — every page leads somewhere
