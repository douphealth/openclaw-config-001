# Internal Link Distribution Patterns

## Goal
Distribute internal links naturally across the article so they support the reader journey instead of clustering awkwardly.

## Good distribution pattern
### Intro / early body
- 1-2 supporting links at most
- use for foundational explainer or adjacent high-value topic

### Mid-body
- 3-4 links spread across workflow, examples, tools, or deeper subtopics
- use the strongest contextual anchors here

### Late body / next steps
- 2-3 links for adjacent commercial pages, supporting guides, or next-step resources

## Rules
- avoid stacking many links in one paragraph
- avoid back-to-back link-heavy paragraphs
- each link should deepen, support, or advance the reader journey
- prefer anchor text that explains what the destination helps with
- if a destination is weak, irrelevant, or non-200, skip it

## Anti-patterns
- dumping five links into the final section
- linking every second sentence
- using generic anchor text like "this article"
- choosing targets by count instead of usefulness
