# E-E-A-T Enhancement Checklist

A systematic checklist for adding Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness signals to editorial content. Designed for affiliate marketing sites where trust directly impacts conversions.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

Score each signal as:
- ✅ **Present & Strong** — signal is clearly visible and credible
- ⚠️ **Weak / Partial** — signal exists but could be stronger
- ❌ **Missing** — signal needs to be added

Target: **80%+ ✅** before publishing.

---

## 1. EXPERIENCE Signals

These prove you (or your authors) have real, first-hand experience with the topic.

### Content-Level Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 1.1 | First-hand account or personal experience mentioned | ☐ | "In our testing…" / "When I used this…" |
| 1.2 | Original screenshots or photos (not stock images) | ☐ | Show the actual product/service in use |
| 1.3 | Specific data from personal use (dates, numbers, results) | ☐ | "After 3 months of daily use…" / "Cost us $X…" |
| 1.4 | Step-by-step walkthrough based on actual process | ☐ | Not regurgitated from other articles |
| 1.5 | Honest limitations or frustrations documented | ☐ | No product is perfect — prove you actually used it |
| 1.6 | Comparison based on hands-on testing | ☐ | "We tested all three and found…" |
| 1.7 | Real case studies or examples with outcomes | ☐ | Named sources or anonymized but specific results |
| 1.8 | Before/after examples where applicable | ☐ | Visual or data-driven proof of change |
| 1.9 | Timeline of experience mentioned | ☐ | "I've been using X for 2+ years" |
| 1.10 | Cost/savings breakdown from actual experience | ☐ | Real numbers, not estimates |

### Implementation Notes
- **For affiliate content:** Include a "How We Test" or "Our Review Process" page linked from each review. Describe your methodology.
- **Screenshots:** Use original screenshots with your own UI elements visible (browser chrome, custom cursors). Cropped, clean stock-quality screenshots look fake.
- **Dates:** Always timestamp when you last tested/used the product. "Tested in March 2026" is better than "Updated 2026."

## 2. EXPERTISE Signals

These demonstrate deep knowledge and authoritative command of the subject.

### Content-Level Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 2.1 | Technical depth beyond surface-level information | ☐ | Cover nuances competitors miss |
| 2.2 | Proper use of industry terminology | ☐ | Correct jargon used naturally, not forced |
| 2.3 | Methodology explained (how conclusions were reached) | ☐ | "We evaluated based on X, Y, Z criteria" |
| 2.4 | Nuanced analysis, not just binary good/bad | ☐ | "It depends on your situation because…" |
| 2.5 | Original frameworks or evaluation criteria | ☐ | Your own scoring system, decision tree, etc. |
| 2.6 | Citations to primary sources | ☐ | Link to official docs, studies, data — not other blogs |
| 2.7 | Coverage of edge cases and advanced scenarios | ☐ | Goes beyond the obvious |
| 2.8 | Current and accurate information | ☐ | Pricing, features, availability verified recently |
| 2.9 | Acknowledgment of uncertainty where it exists | ☐ | "The data on this is limited" is honest expertise |
| 2.10 | Clear, well-structured argumentation | ☐ | Logic flows; conclusions follow from evidence |

### Author-Level Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 2.11 | Author bio with relevant credentials | ☐ | Linked from the article |
| 2.12 | Author profile page with expertise area | ☐ | Dedicated page listing credentials + other articles |
| 2.13 | Author's other published work in same domain | ☐ | Internal links to related author content |
| 2.14 | Professional certifications or degrees (if relevant) | ☐ | Only mention if genuinely relevant |
| 2.15 | Years of experience in the field mentioned | ☐ | "With 8 years in [field]…" |

## 3. AUTHORITY Signals

These establish your site and authors as recognized voices in the space.

### Site-Level Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 3.1 | External references and citations from credible sources | ☐ | Academic, industry, government sources |
| 3.2 | Original research or data published | ☐ | Surveys, benchmarks, studies conducted by your team |
| 3.3 | Expert quotes or interviews included | ☐ | Named experts with credentials |
| 3.4 | Links to official sources (manufacturer, regulatory) | ☐ | Primary sources, not secondhand reporting |
| 3.5 | Awards, mentions, or recognition listed | ☐ | "As featured in…" / "Awarded…" |
| 3.6 | Industry partnerships or affiliations disclosed | ☐ | Transparent about relationships |
| 3.7 | Comprehensive "About" page | ☐ | Site mission, team, methodology |
| 3.8 | Contact information easily accessible | ☐ | Email, physical address, or contact form |
| 3.9 | Editorial policy / review methodology page | ☐ | Explains how content is created and verified |
| 3.10 | Consistent publishing history | ☐ | Regular content updates signal active authority |

### Content-Level Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 3.11 | Internal links to related authoritative content | ☐ | Build topical authority through hub-and-spoke |
| 3.12 | External links to high-authority references | ☐ | 3-8 credible outbound links per article |
| 3.13 | Original diagrams, charts, or infographics | ☐ | Created by your team, not borrowed |
| 3.14 | Comprehensive coverage of the topic | ☐ | Longest doesn't mean best, but gaps hurt authority |
| 3.15 | Clear sourcing for all claims and statistics | ☐ | Every number has a source |

## 4. TRUST Signals

These make readers (and Google) trust your content and recommendations.

### Transparency Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 4.1 | Affiliate disclosure prominently placed | ☐ | Above the fold or in clear view, not buried |
| 4.2 | "How We Make Money" page | ☐ | Full transparency on revenue model |
| 4.3 | Review methodology explained | ☐ | How products were selected, tested, ranked |
| 4.4 | Conflict of interest disclosure | ☐ | "We received a free copy…" is better than hiding it |
| 4.5 | Clear distinction between editorial and sponsored | ☐ | Label sponsored content explicitly |

### Accuracy Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 4.6 | Last updated date visible | ☐ | Top of article, not hidden in footer |
| 4.7 | "Fact checked" or "Reviewed by" badge | ☐ | With reviewer name and credentials |
| 4.8 | Corrections policy available | ☐ | How you handle errors |
| 4.9 | Specific, verifiable claims (not vague generalizations) | ☐ | "Save $47/month" > "Save money" |
| 4.10 | Balanced perspective (not everything is "amazing") | ☐ | Real pros AND cons |

### Safety & Compliance Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 4.11 | Privacy policy accessible | ☐ | Especially if collecting emails |
| 4.12 | Terms of service available | ☐ | For any transactional pages |
| 4.13 | GDPR/CCPA compliance for data collection | ☐ | Cookie consent, data handling |
| 4.14 | Accessible contact for complaints/corrections | ☐ | Not a black hole |
| 4.15 | SSL/HTTPS on all pages | ☐ | Basic but essential |

## 5. YMYL Compliance (Your Money or Your Life)

For finance, health, legal, safety, or major purchase topics — the bar is much higher.

### Mandatory YMYL Signals
| # | Signal | Present? | Notes |
|---|--------|----------|-------|
| 5.1 | Author has verifiable credentials in the field | ☐ | CFP for finance, MD/RN for health, JD for legal |
| 5.2 | Medical/legal/financial disclaimer included | ☐ | "This is not financial advice. Consult a professional." |
| 5.3 | Content reviewed by a qualified professional | ☐ | "Medically reviewed by Dr. X" |
| 5.4 | All claims supported by primary sources | ☐ | Studies, government data, official statements |
| 5.5 | No misleading absolute claims | ☐ | "Can help" > "Will cure" |
| 5.6 | Risks and limitations clearly stated | ☐ | Not buried in fine print |
| 5.7 | "Last medically/financially reviewed" date | ☐ | Different from "last updated" |
| 5.8 | Regular review schedule established | ☐ | YMYL content needs quarterly+ review |
| 5.9 | Links to professional help resources | ☐ | Helplines, directories, official resources |
| 5.10 | No exploitation of fear or urgency | ☐ | Inform, don't manipulate |

### YMYL Content Categories
- **Finance:** Investment advice, tax guidance, insurance, loans, crypto, budgeting
- **Health:** Medical conditions, treatments, medications, supplements, fitness claims
- **Legal:** Legal advice, rights, regulations, compliance
- **Safety:** Product safety, emergency procedures, security
- **Major Purchases:** Cars, homes, education — anything with significant financial commitment

## Quick Enhancement Actions

When an article is missing E-E-A-T signals, here are the fastest fixes:

### 5-Minute Fixes
1. Add a "Last Updated: [Date]" banner at the top
2. Add an affiliate disclosure if missing
3. Add specific numbers/data where vague claims exist
4. Add "In our experience" framing to existing claims
5. Link to your "About" and "Review Methodology" pages

### 15-Minute Fixes
6. Add an author bio box at the end
7. Add 2-3 original screenshots
8. Add 3-5 outbound links to credible sources
9. Write a "How We Tested" section
10. Add balanced pros AND cons to any recommendation

### 30-Minute Fixes
11. Write a dedicated "Our Testing Methodology" section
12. Add a "Reviewed by [Expert Name]" badge
13. Create original comparison data from your testing
14. Write FAQ answers that demonstrate depth
15. Add a "Who This Is/Isn't For" section to recommendations

## Scoring

After completing the checklist:

| Score | Rating | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| 90-100% ✅ | Excellent | Ready to publish |
| 75-89% ✅ | Good | Address remaining gaps |
| 60-74% ⚠️ | Needs work | Significant E-E-A-T gaps |
| Below 60% ❌ | Weak | Major overhaul needed |

**Minimum for YMYL content:** 85% ✅ required before publishing.
**Minimum for non-YMYL affiliate content:** 75% ✅ required before publishing.
