# Semantic Gap Patterns

Learned patterns of missing entities, keywords, and concepts by topic/type.
Updated by the self-improvement protocol after each enhancement.

## How to Use This File
When diagnosing an article, scan this list for the article’s topic.
If you see a pattern, check whether those entities/concepts are present.
Add any missing ones naturally into the content.

## Format
**Topic**: [missing entity/concept 1], [missing entity/concept 2], ...

## Template for New Topics
If the article’s topic isn’t listed, use this structure to brainstorm gaps:
- **Core concepts**: What are the 3-5 fundamental ideas everyone agrees on?
- **Key metrics**: What numbers do experts track to measure success?
- **Tools & platforms**: What software, apps, or hardware do practitioners use?
- **Process steps**: What is the typical workflow or sequence of actions?
- **Real-world examples**: What specific case studies or stories illustrate the concept?
- **Frequently asked questions**: What do beginners always ask about this topic?
- **Common mistakes**: What do people get wrong when trying to apply this?
- **Advanced techniques**: What do experts do that beginners don’t know about?
- **Tools of the trade**: What specific brands or models do professionals prefer?
- **Historical context**: How has the understanding of this topic evolved over time?

## Adding Missing Entities Naturally
Once you identify a gap:
1. **Find the right section**: Where does this concept logically belong?
2. **Use a natural lead-in**: “For example,” “As seen in,” “Research shows,” “In my experience,”
3. **Add the entity with context**: Don’t just drop the term — explain why it matters
4. **Consider formatting**: Bold for key terms, statistics in blockquotes, examples in callout boxes
5. **Check flow**: Read the sentence aloud — does it sound natural or forced?
