# Editorial Post Enhancement

## Purpose
Turn an article into a stronger editorial asset by improving search fit, publishing quality, internal links, helpful structure, and trust signals without introducing cannibalization or empty SEO theater.

## Use this when
- improving an existing blog post or article
- adding internal links, FAQ coverage, media, or better structure
- strengthening SEO, E-E-A-T, or SERP usefulness
- closing topic/entity gaps against the current SERP
- optimizing title, slug, meta direction, or publishing quality

## Do NOT use this for
- net-new article creation (→ `conversion-copywriting`)
- content strategy planning (→ `content-strategy-planning`)
- site-wide SEO audits (→ `seo-audit-playbook`)

## Do this
1. Define the target keyword/topic and current article role.
2. Check for cannibalization first.
3. Check the current SERP and identify intent, content patterns, and obvious gaps.
4. Improve answer-first structure and on-page clarity.
5. Add or improve internal links, media, FAQ coverage, references, and conversion elements when relevant.
6. Improve publishing quality without adding decorative junk.
7. Validate that changes strengthen the page without harming clarity, performance, or differentiation.

## Core rules
- Always check cannibalization before major optimization.
- Improve for reader usefulness first, then search visibility.
- Do not add visual or SEO elements that do not improve the page.
- Distribute internal links naturally; do not clump them.
- Never treat static HTML inspection alone as proof of final publishing quality when rendered behavior matters.

## Strong enhancement areas
- answer-first intro
- title/meta/slug direction
- logical H2/H3 structure
- entity/subtopic gap closure
- contextual internal links
- relevant media
- FAQ section
- references and trust signals
- stronger CTA or next-step flow when appropriate

## Checks and common mistakes
- optimizing the wrong page because cannibalization was skipped
- adding FAQ/schema/media without improving the page’s actual usefulness
- overstuffing keywords or entities
- bunching internal links in one section
- confusing visual complexity with quality
- forgetting to verify important links or publishing behavior

## Output contract
**Artifact:** enhanced article or prioritized enhancement plan
**Evidence:** gap closure, structural improvements, internal-link/media changes, and readiness rationale
**Decision:** ready to publish/update, needs more work, or blocked by cannibalization/intent issues
**Next:** publish, verify live rendering, monitor performance, or route to a more specific execution skill
