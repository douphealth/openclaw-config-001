# Analytics Reporting

## Purpose
Turn messy metrics into decision-ready reporting with clear caveats, meaningful KPI selection, and recommendations that actually change what someone should do next.

## Use this when
- analyzing business, marketing, product, or operational data
- producing KPI summaries, performance reports, or cohort insights
- planning dashboard structure or reporting cadence
- interpreting attribution, trends, or funnel data
- turning raw numbers into business decisions

## Do NOT use this for
- experiment design or A/B test structuring (→ `experiment-tracking`)
- fixing broken tracking or instrumentation (→ `tracking-measurement`)
- launch readiness checks (→ `launch-readiness-audit`)
- technical SEO audits (→ `seo-audit-playbook`)
- creating or editing content (→ `editorial-post-enhancement`)

## Do this
1. Define the business question before touching the numbers.
2. Define data scope: source, date range, granularity, and obvious quality risks.
3. Select 3-5 primary KPIs that map directly to a decision.
4. Separate leading indicators from lagging indicators.
5. Validate data quality before writing conclusions.
6. Separate descriptive facts from interpretation.
7. Highlight top drivers, likely business impact, and major unknowns.
8. End with prioritized actions, not just observations.

## KPI rules
- Every KPI must map to a decision.
- Prefer ratios/rates over raw totals when comparing segments.
- Avoid vanity metrics unless you can explain why they matter.
- Use a meaningful baseline: prior period, target, forecast, or cohort average.

## Analysis rules
- Do not present noise as a real trend.
- Flag seasonality, one-time events, and pipeline changes that may distort interpretation.
- Use absolute numbers for magnitude and percentages for direction.
- State confidence level when the evidence is weak or incomplete.
- If attribution is weak, say so plainly.

## Dashboard rule
If designing a dashboard, structure it as:
1. headline KPI row
2. driver breakdowns
3. drill-down detail
4. one clear next action or CTA

## Checks and common mistakes
- Do not bury caveats after bold conclusions.
- Do not confuse correlation with causation.
- Do not use 10 metrics when 4 would make the point.
- Do not produce executive summaries with no action attached.
- Do not report totals without context or comparison.

## Output contract
**Artifact:** report, KPI summary, dashboard plan, or decision memo
**Evidence:** source scope, comparison periods, formulas/definitions where needed, confidence level, and explicit caveats
**Decision:** prioritized actions with quantified or reasoned impact
**Next:** implementation, follow-up analysis, dashboard build, or data cleanup
