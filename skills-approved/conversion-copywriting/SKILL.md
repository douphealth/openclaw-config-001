# Conversion Copywriting

## Purpose
Write clear, persuasive page copy that moves a specific audience toward a specific action — using proven persuasion frameworks, practical examples, and enterprise-grade publishing quality.

## Use this when
- writing new marketing copy for a page or offer
- strengthening headlines, subheads, CTAs, or section copy
- sharpening value propositions and reducing fluff
- creating conversion-focused copy in Hormozi/Ferriss style (direct, practical, proof-driven)
- optimizing page messaging before design or CRO work
- rewriting sections that feel generic, vague, or "SaaS sludge"

## Do NOT use this for
- planning content strategy or keyword research (→ `content-strategy-planning`)
- line-by-line editing of existing drafts (→ `copy-editing-sweeps`)
- upgrading published articles for SEO/internal links (→ `editorial-post-enhancement`)
- offer definition or positioning strategy (→ `offer-positioning`)
- email sequences or drip campaigns (→ `lifecycle-email-sequences`)
- funnel structure or page architecture (→ `service-funnel-architecture`)

## Self-Improvement Protocol

After each copywriting session:
1. **Framework accuracy:** Which persuasion framework was used? Did it fit the page type?
2. **Clarity audit:** Circle every sentence with 20+ words. Can they be split?
3. **Specificity check:** Flag every vague claim. Can it be replaced with a number or example?
4. **CTA strength:** Rate each CTA 1-5 for clarity, urgency, and specificity.
5. **Proof density:** Count proof elements (testimonials, stats, case studies). Aim for 1 per 300 words.
6. **Generic language scan:** Search for "streamline", "synergy", "leverage", "robust", "seamless". Replace with concrete descriptions.
7. **Learn patterns:** If a headline/CTA variant outperformed, add it to `references/winning-patterns.md`.

## Do this

### Phase 1: Page & Audience Lock (5 minutes)

**Page type determines everything:**
| Page Type | Primary Goal | Key Emotion | Proof Type |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Homepage | Route to next step | Trust + curiosity | Social proof, logos, stats |
| Landing page | Single conversion action | Urgency + desire | Testimonials, before/after, demo |
| Pricing page | Choose a plan | Confidence + clarity | Comparison tables, guarantees, FAQs |
| Product page | Buy or sign up | Excitement + trust | Feature→benefit, screenshots, reviews |
| Feature page | Understand value | Curiosity + clarity | Use cases, screenshots, outcomes |
| Offer page | Buy now | Scarcity + urgency | Limited-time proof, ROI calculator |
| CTA section | Click | Clarity + low friction | Single compelling reason |

**Audience questions (answer before writing a word):**
1. What is their **#1 pain point** right now?
2. What **outcome** do they desperately want?
3. What **objections** would stop them from acting?
4. What **proof** would make them believe this works?
5. What **language** do they use to describe their problem? (exact phrases from reviews, support tickets, sales calls)

### Phase 2: Value Proposition Frame

Use the Hormozi **Grand Slam Offer** framework:

```
Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Time Delay × Effort/Sacrifice
```

**Translate into copy:**
- **Dream Outcome:** "What's the BEST possible result?" → Lead with this
- **Likelihood:** "Why should I believe this works for ME?" → Proof, case studies, specificity
- **Time Delay:** "How FAST will I see results?" → Timeframes, milestones
- **Effort/Sacrifice:** "How HARD is this?" → Done-for-you, templates, automation

**Example (SaaS tool):**
- ❌ Generic: "Streamline your marketing workflow"
- ✅ Hormozi-style: "Launch 3x more campaigns in half the time — our customers average 14 campaigns/month vs their old 5, and most get there within their first week"

### Phase 3: Page Copy Structure

Use or adapt this default flow:

#### 1. Headline (The Hook)
**Formula:** [Specific Outcome] + [Timeframe/Proof] + [Mechanism]

Examples:
- "Double Your Email Open Rates in 30 Days With the Segmentation Framework Used by 2,000+ B2B Marketers"
- "Go From Zero to $10K/Month in Recurring Revenue — The Exact Playbook 137 Founders Used Last Year"
- "Stop Wasting Ad Spend: The 5-Minute Audit That Found $47K in Wasted Budget for Our Clients"

**Generate 3 options:** One bold claim, one proof-led, one question-led.

#### 2. Subheadline (The Clarifier)
Expand on the headline. Answer: "What exactly is this and who is it for?"

Example: "A step-by-step email segmentation system built for SaaS teams with 1,000-50,000 subscribers. No marketing degree required."

#### 3. Primary CTA (First Ask)
Make it specific, low-friction, and benefit-oriented:
- ❌ "Submit" / "Get Started" / "Learn More"
- ✅ "Get the Free Segmentation Template" / "Start Your 14-Day Trial — No Credit Card" / "See It Live in 2 Minutes"

#### 4. Proof/Trust (Earn the Right to Continue)
Place immediately after the CTA:
- Logos of recognizable customers (even 3-5 help)
- One specific testimonial with numbers
- A single killer stat ("Used by 4,200 teams" or "Average 34% improvement in Week 1")

#### 5. Problem Framing (Agitate the Pain)
Describe the reader's current reality in their language:
- Use specific scenarios they recognize
- Quantify the pain when possible ("Every mis-qualified lead costs you $X and Y hours")
- Name the frustration they haven't articulated yet

**Hormozi pattern:** "Here's the thing nobody tells you about [topic]: [painful truth]. Most people try [common wrong approach] and end up [bad outcome]."

#### 6. Solution / Benefits (Show the Path)
Connect features → outcomes → personal/business value:

| Feature | Outcome | Value |
|---------|---------|-------|
| "AI-powered segmentation" | "Segments update automatically" | "No more manual list management — saves 4+ hours/week" |
| "One-click integrations" | "Connects to your CRM in 60 seconds" | "Start using your data immediately, not after 3 weeks of IT setup" |
| "Pre-built templates" | "Copy-paste campaigns ready to go" | "Launch your first campaign today, not next month" |

#### 7. How It Works (3-5 Steps)
Reduce perceived complexity. Use numbered steps:
1. Connect your data source (60 seconds)
2. Pick a pre-built template or start from scratch
3. Launch and watch results in real-time

#### 8. Objection Handling / FAQ
Address the top 3-5 objections directly:
- "What if I'm not technical?" → "If you can use email, you can use this. Setup takes 5 minutes."
- "How is this different from [competitor]?" → "[Specific differentiator]. See the full comparison."
- "What if it doesn't work for my niche?" → "Here are [X] customers in [your niche] already using it."
- "Is it worth the price?" → "The average customer saves $[X]/month in wasted time. This costs $[Y]."

#### 9. Final CTA (Last Push)
Reinforce the outcome, add urgency if genuine:
- "Start your free trial today — most customers see results within their first week"
- "Join 4,200+ teams who stopped guessing and started segmenting"
- "Limited spots for onboarding this month — claim yours"

### Phase 4: Copy Polish (Hormozi/Ferriss Style)

#### Clarity Rules
- One idea per sentence. If a sentence has "and" linking two ideas, split it.
- One main point per section. If a section wanders, cut or split.
- Sentences under 20 words when possible.
- Paragraphs under 4 lines on mobile (2-3 sentences max).

#### Specificity Rules
- Every claim needs a number, name, or example.
- Replace adjectives with data: "powerful" → "handles 10M requests/second"
- Use concrete timeframes: "fast" → "results in 14 days"
- Name real people/companies when you have permission.

#### Voice Rules (Hormozi/Ferriss)
- Write like you're explaining to a smart friend, not writing a corporate brochure.
- Use "you" more than "we" — the copy is about them, not you.
- Short sentences for impact. Long sentences for rhythm. Mix both.
- Bold key takeaways, not random words.
- No jargon without immediate explanation.
- No hedging: say it directly or cut it.

#### HTML Presentation (When Publishing Surface Allows)
Use modern HTML elements to make copy scannable and engaging:
- **Summary boxes** at the top: "What you'll learn" / "TL;DR" / "Key takeaways"
- **Comparison tables** for pricing or features
- **Checklists** for implementation steps
- **Callout boxes** for tips, warnings, and key insights
- **Pull quotes** for powerful testimonials or data points
- **Visual separators** between major sections
- **Icon lists** for benefits/features (use emoji or simple unicode: ✓, →, ★)
- **Collapsible sections** for FAQ or detailed objections

### Phase 5: Quality Gates

Before declaring copy complete, run these checks:

1. **Clarity test:** Read every sentence aloud. If you stumble, rewrite.
2. **Jargon scan:** Search for corporate buzzwords. Replace with plain English.
3. **Specificity audit:** Count vague claims. Target: <10% of sentences.
4. **Proof density:** At least 1 proof element per 300 words.
5. **CTA clarity:** Every CTA answers "what happens when I click?"
6. **Mobile check:** Does the copy flow on a phone? Short paragraphs, clear hierarchy.
7. **One-idea-per-section:** Can you summarize each section in one sentence? If not, split.
8. **Reader focus:** Count "you/your" vs "we/our/us". Target: 2:1 ratio (reader-focused).

## Core rules
- Clarity beats cleverness. Always.
- Specificity beats vagueness. Always.
- Benefits beat feature lists. Always.
- Honest proof beats hype. Always.
- One page pushes one primary action.
- Never invent numbers, testimonials, or claims.
- If the copy could fit 50 other products, it is not done.
- Write for humans first. SEO, GEO, and AEO structure should never make copy sound machine-written.
- The strongest pages feel designed, not dumped — use HTML structure that improves readability.

## Default page flow
Use or adapt this structure:
1. Headline (3 options)
2. Subheadline
3. Primary CTA
4. Proof / trust signal
5. Problem / missed-opportunity framing
6. Solution / benefits
7. How it works (3-5 steps)
8. Objections / FAQ
9. Final CTA

## Output contract
**Artifact:** Conversion copy for target page(s) with 2-3 headline options and 2-3 CTA options
**Evidence:** Before/after comparison, persuasion framework used, proof elements included
**Decision:** Copy approved for implementation, needs revision, or requires more proof/research
**Next:** A/B test headline/CTA variants, implement with design, or iterate on specific sections

## Checks and common mistakes
- Generic claims ("streamline your workflow") without specifics → rewrite with numbers
- One section pitching multiple unrelated ideas → split into separate sections
- CTA buried behind unnecessary scene-setting → move CTA above the fold
- Corporate sludge when plain English works → rewrite in conversational tone
- Brand voice confused with vagueness or filler → keep voice, cut filler
- No proof elements → add testimonials, stats, or case studies
- Feature lists without outcomes → translate every feature into "which means..."
- Ignoring mobile layout → test on phone viewport
