# Persuasion Frameworks

Proven frameworks for writing conversion copy. Choose based on page type and audience awareness level.

## Framework 1: Hormozi Grand Slam Offer

**Best for:** Offers, landing pages, pricing pages, sales pages

```
Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Time Delay × Effort/Sacrifice
```

**How to use in copy:**
1. **Dream Outcome:** Lead with the BEST possible result. Be specific.
   - "Launch 3x more campaigns in half the time"
   - "Go from $0 to $10K MRR in 90 days"
2. **Likelihood:** Stack proof. Case studies, testimonials, guarantees.
   - "137 founders used this exact playbook last year"
   - "Average customer sees results in their first week"
3. **Time Delay:** Compress perceived wait time with milestones.
   - "First results in 48 hours"
   - "Week 1: setup. Week 2: first campaign. Week 3: first revenue"
4. **Effort/Sacrifice:** Reduce friction. Done-for-you, templates, automation.
   - "Copy-paste templates — no design skills needed"
   - "We handle setup. You approve and launch."

**Copy template:**
"Get [dream outcome] in [timeframe] — even if [common objection]. [Proof element]. [CTA]."

## Framework 2: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

**Best for:** Pain-aware audiences, problem-first landing pages, email copy

```
Problem → Agitate → Solve
```

**How to use:**
1. **Problem:** Name their pain in their own language.
   - "You're spending $5K/month on ads but can't tell which ones actually drive revenue."
2. **Agitate:** Make the pain feel worse. Show consequences.
   - "Every month you wait, you're burning budget on campaigns that will never convert — and missing the ones that would."
3. **Solve:** Present your solution as the relief.
   - "Our attribution dashboard shows exactly which ad drove which sale. One click. Real-time data."

**Copy template:**
"[Painful problem]. [Worse consequence if ignored]. [Your solution fixes this]. [CTA]."

## Framework 3: AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

**Best for:** Cold audiences, awareness-stage pages, homepage hero sections

```
Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
```

**How to use:**
1. **Attention:** Bold headline. Pattern interrupt. Surprising stat.
   - "Most companies waste 40% of their ad budget. Here's how to find yours."
2. **Interest:** Explain why this matters to THEM. New information.
   - "We analyzed 12,000 campaigns and found the 3 mistakes that cause 90% of waste."
3. **Desire:** Show the transformation. Paint the after picture.
   - "Imagine knowing exactly which ads work — and cutting your CPA by 30% in the first month."
4. **Action:** Clear, specific CTA.
   - "Get your free ad audit — takes 5 minutes, saves you thousands."

## Framework 4: Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

**Best for:** Transformation stories, case-study-style pages, service offerings

```
Before (painful state) → After (desired state) → Bridge (your solution)
```

**How to use:**
1. **Before:** Describe their current frustrating reality.
   - "Right now, you're managing campaigns across 4 platforms, manually updating spreadsheets, and still missing opportunities."
2. **After:** Paint the ideal future.
   - "Imagine one dashboard showing all your campaigns, auto-optimized bids, and alerts when something needs your attention."
3. **Bridge:** Show how your product/service gets them there.
   - "That's exactly what CampaignOS does. Connect your accounts in 2 minutes and let the AI handle the rest."

## Framework 5: The 4Ps (Promise-Prove-Push-Picture)

**Best for:** Long-form sales pages, webinar registrations, high-ticket offers

```
Promise → Prove → Picture → Push
```

**How to use:**
1. **Promise:** Make a bold, specific promise.
   - "Double your email revenue in 90 days — guaranteed."
2. **Prove:** Back it up with evidence.
   - "Here's how 847 companies did exactly that, with real screenshots and data."
3. **Picture:** Help them visualize the result.
   - "Picture this: you check your dashboard Monday morning and see $12K in email revenue from last week — on autopilot."
4. **Push:** Create urgency or scarcity.
   - "Enrollment closes Friday. Only 30 spots left for this quarter's cohort."

## Framework 6: FAB (Features-Advantages-Benefits)

**Best for:** Product pages, feature descriptions, SaaS pricing pages

```
Feature → Advantage → Benefit
```

**How to use:**
1. **Feature:** What the product has.
   - "AI-powered segmentation engine"
2. **Advantage:** What it does better than alternatives.
   - "Updates segments in real-time instead of overnight batch processing"
3. **Benefit:** What it means for the reader.
   - "Your campaigns always target the right people — even when customer behavior changes mid-campaign"

**Always lead with the benefit, then explain how:**
- ❌ "Our AI-powered segmentation engine updates segments in real-time, meaning your campaigns target the right people."
- ✅ "Your campaigns always target the right people — even when behavior changes mid-campaign. Our AI engine updates segments in real-time, not overnight."

## Framework 7: Cialdini's Principles (Choose 2-3 per Page)

**Best for:** Any conversion page — select the 2-3 most relevant principles

| Principle | Copy Technique | Example |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Reciprocity | Give value first | "Free audit template — no signup required" |
| Social Proof | Show others using it | "Join 4,200+ teams" / testimonial |
| Authority | Demonstrate expertise | "Built by ex-Google engineers" / credentials |
| Scarcity | Limited supply | "Only 12 spots remaining" |
| Urgency | Time pressure | "Price increases Friday" (only if true) |
| Consistency | Small yes → big yes | "Start free, upgrade when ready" |
| Liking | Be relatable | Personal story, shared struggle |

## Choosing a Framework

| Page Type | Best Framework | Runner-Up |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Homepage | AIDA | PAS |
| Landing page | PAS or Hormozi | BAB |
| Pricing page | FAB + Social Proof | Hormozi |
| Product page | FAB | BAB |
| Sales page | Hormozi or 4Ps | PAS |
| Offer page | Hormozi + Scarcity | 4Ps |
| About page | BAB | Authority + Liking |

## Framework Stacking

For high-stakes pages, combine frameworks:
- **Hormozi + PAS:** Lead with PAS (agitate pain), then present Hormozi offer as the solution
- **AIDA + FAB:** Use AIDA for the page flow, FAB for individual feature sections
- **BAB + Cialdini:** Tell the transformation story, then add social proof/authority to validate

## Self-Critique (After Choosing a Framework)

1. Does the framework match the audience's awareness level?
2. Does the framework fit the page's conversion goal?
3. Is the framework applied consistently throughout, not abandoned halfway?
4. Are the proof elements sufficient for the claims being made?
5. Does the copy flow naturally, or does the framework feel forced?
