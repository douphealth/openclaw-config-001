# Winning Copy Patterns

Learned high-performing copy patterns from past sessions. Updated by the self-improvement protocol.

## SaaS Landing Pages

### Headlines That Convert
- "Launch [X]x More [Thing] in Half the Time" → specificity + speed + outcome
- "[Number] [Audience] Already Use [Product] to [Result]" → social proof + outcome
- "The [Adjective] Way to [Outcome] Without [Pain Point]" → mechanism + relief

### CTA Patterns That Work
- "Get [Specific Resource] — Free" (low friction, clear value)
- "Start Your [X]-Day Trial — No Credit Card" (risk reversal)
- "See It Live in [Short Timeframe]" (instant gratification)
- "Get the Same [Template/System] [Number] [Audience] Use" (social proof + value)

### Proof Placement
- Immediately after headline: logos + one killer stat
- Mid-page: 2-3 detailed testimonials with specific results
- Before final CTA: summary of proof ("Join 4,200+ teams who...")
- Avoid: proof only at the bottom (too late)

## E-commerce / Product Pages

### Headlines That Convert
- "[Number]+ [People/Teams] Switched from [Competitor] — Here's Why" → curiosity + social proof
- "[Outcome] Guaranteed — Or Your Money Back" → risk reversal + outcome
- "The Last [Product Category] You'll Ever Buy" → longevity + commitment

### CTA Patterns That Work
- "Add to Cart — Ships Today" (instant + specific)
- "Get [X]% Off — Today Only" (scarcity + value)
- "Try It Risk-Free for [X] Days" (guarantee)

## Service / Agency Pages

### Headlines That Convert
- "We [Specific Outcome] for [Audience] — [Timeframe]" → specificity + audience + speed
- "From [Before State] to [After State] in [Timeframe]" → transformation + timeline
- "Stop [Pain Point]. Start [Desired Outcome]." → contrast + clarity

### CTA Patterns That Work
- "Book Your Free [Audit/Strategy Call]" (specific + low risk)
- "Get a Custom [Plan/Strategy] in [X] Days" (specific + fast)
- "See If We're a Fit — 15-Minute Call" (low commitment)

## Section Copy Patterns

### Problem Section
- "Here's what nobody tells you about [topic]: [painful truth]"
- "Most [audience] try [common approach] and end up [bad outcome]"
- "If you're [doing specific thing], you're probably [losing/missing specific thing]"

### Solution Section
- "What if you could [desired outcome] — without [pain point]?"
- "That's exactly what [product] does. [One-sentence mechanism]."
- "Here's how it works: [3 numbered steps]"

### Objection Handling
- "You might be thinking: '[objection]'. Here's why that's not a problem: [answer]."
- "I know what you're thinking: '[objection]'. [Specific proof it's not true]."
- "Before you say '[objection]', consider this: [counter-evidence]."

## HTML Presentation Patterns

```html

```

## Anti-Patterns (What Kills Conversions)

- ❌ "We're passionate about..." (nobody cares about your passion)
- ❌ "Industry-leading" (says every company ever)
- ❌ "Best-in-class" (meaningless without proof)
- ❌ "Synergy" / "Leverage" / "Robust" / "Seamless" (corporate sludge)
- ❌ Long paragraphs before CTA (mobile users won't scroll that far)
- ❌ Multiple competing CTAs (decision paralysis)
- ❌ CTA text that doesn't describe the action ("Submit", "Click Here")
- ❌ Proof only at the bottom (should be throughout)
- ❌ Generic testimonials: "Great product!" (need specifics: numbers, results, context)
- ❌ Stock photos instead of real screenshots/team photos

## Learning Protocol

When a copy variant outperforms:
1. Record the exact wording in this file
2. Note the page type, audience, and context
3. Tag with the persuasion framework used
4. Add to the relevant section above
5. Consider: can this pattern be abstracted into a reusable template?
